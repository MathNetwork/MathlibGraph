\begin{abstract}
Formal mathematics libraries are deterministic: every dependency is machine-verified, and the compiled artifact contains no ambiguity. Yet these libraries are built by humans through an uncertain process of exploration, organization, and judgment. We analyze the network structure of Mathlib, the largest unified library of formalized mathematics, and show that the deterministic product preserves measurable traces of the uncertain process that created it. The organizational conventions, cognitive habits, and importance judgments of human contributors are encoded in the dependency topology and can be distinguished from the constraints imposed by mathematical logic itself.
\end{abstract}
